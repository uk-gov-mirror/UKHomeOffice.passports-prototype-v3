Photos for adults

Use a plain light-coloured background

  • no texture or patterns
  • no objects behind you

  • Plain light-coloured background

  • Textured background

  • Object in background

Even lighting and no shadows

  • balanced light - no shadows on your face or behind you
  • it’s best to use natural sunlight, for example facing a window

  • Even lighting and no shadow

  • Shadow on face

  • Shadow behind head

Stand in the right position

  • stand 0.5 metres (1.5 feet) away from your background (this reduces shadow)
  • the person taking the photo should stand 1.5 metres (5 feet) from you
  • include your head, shoulders and upper body in the photo
  • don’t crop your photo - it will be done for you

Plain expression and face in full view

  • face front on to the camera
  • don’t smile or frown - eyes open and mouth closed
  • your photo should be a good likeness and taken in the last month

  • Plain expression

  • Smiling

  • Looking away from camera

No headwear

  • unless it’s for religious or medical reasons

  • Religious headwear

  • Fashion hair accessories

  • Fashion headwear

Eyes fully visible

  • hair away from your face and eyes
  • take your glasses off (if you can)

If you need to keep glasses on:

  • your eyes must be visible without any glare or reflections
  • no sunglasses or tinted glasses

  • Eyes fully visible

  • Glasses covering eyes

  • Hair covering eyes

Photos for babies and children

Use a plain light-coloured background

  • no texture or patterns
  • children must be on their own in the photo
  • babies must not be holding toys or dummies

Children under 1:

  • you can support their head with your hand but your hand must not be visible

  • Plain light-coloured background

  • Holding a toy

  • Object in background

Even lighting and no shadows

  • balanced light - no shadows on your child’s face or behind them
  • it’s best to use natural sunlight, for example facing a window

  • Even lighting and no shadow

  • Shadow on face

  • Shadow behind head

Stand in the right position

When you take the photo of your child:

  • they should stand 0.5 metres (1.5 feet) from the background (this reduces shadow)
  • you need to stand far enough away so that their head, shoulders and upper body are included in the photo
  • don’t crop the photo - it will be done for you

Children under 1:

  • lay them on a plain light-coloured sheet and take the photo from above

  • Plain light-coloured background

  • Dark background

  • Holding a toy

Plain expression and face in full view

  • face front on to the camera
  • no smile or frown - eyes open and mouth closed
  • your child’s photo should be a good likeness and taken in the last month

Children under 6:

  • don’t have to look directly at the camera
  • don’t need to have a plain expression

  • Plain expression

  • Smiling

  • Looking away from camera

No headwear

  • unless it’s for religious or medical reasons

  • Religious headwear

  • Fashion headwear

  • Fashion hair accessories

Eyes fully visible

  • hair away from your face and eyes
  • take your glasses off (if you can)

Children under 1:

  • don’t have to have their eyes open

If your child needs to keep their glasses on:

  • their eyes must be visible without any glare or reflections

  • Eyes fully visible

  • Glasses covering eyes

  • Hair covering eyes
