Her Majesty’s Passport Office is introducing a way for customers to retrieve a digital photo when they apply for their passport online.

Currently, customers can only upload a digital photo stored on their own device or provided by a photo shop on a memory stick, email or CD. With this new solution, booths and shops in the UK will be able to give customers a photo code with their printed passport photos. When customers apply online, they can use this code to retrieve their digital passport photo taken at a booth or by a photographer. This provides greater choice for our customers.

Suppliers of digital passport photos will need to establish a secure process for storing and transferring their customers’ digital photos. Her Majesty’s Passport Office will continue to check that all photos meet the rules for UK passports.

Providing digital passport photo codes – advice for photo shops

To enable your customers to use this new service, you need to:

  • host your digital passport photos securely on the internet
  • convert the full HTTPS internet address (URL) into a shortened version
  • provide the shortened internet address to customers as a photo code

Technical instructions

  1. Upload the digital photo to a publically accessible host on the internet. It must be possible to access the photo via a secure shortened internet address (URL) from anywhere on the internet.
  2. The photo must be a JPEG between 50KB and 10MB. The ideal image size is 3MB. It must be possible to retrieve the photo in less than 30 seconds.
  3. The photo must meet the rules for digital passport photos.
  4. The full HTTPS internet address (URL) of the photo must be converted into a shortened version. URL shortening services are available.
  5. The shortened version must follow the format of sub-domain (1-6 characters), top-level-domain (2-3 characters), and path (6-8 characters), for example photo.co/yh8Y6K2s
  6. Provide the shortened URL to your customer. Make sure you describe it as a ‘photo code’ and include details of how customers can contact you if there’s a problem with their digital photo.
  7. Implement the optional secure web token to restrict photo retrieval to HMPO only. HMPO use a JWS (JSON Web Signature) in the Authorization header, signed with our private key that can be authenticated using our published public key. The ‘sub’ in the JWS token is the HTTPS URL of the photo code and can be checked.
