You may be asked to come for an interview at a passport office if you’ve never had a UK passport before, or your old passport expired some time ago.

The interview helps us confirm who you are and that the passport application belongs to you.

It’s an important part of protecting your identity. It stops other people applying for a passport in your name.

What happens at the interview

Your appointment will last about 30 minutes.

We’ll ask you some questions that someone trying to steal your identity may not know. We’ll also check your photo looks like you.

{% call hmpoInsetText() %}

You won’t get your new passport on the day. We’ll carry out final checks on your application before we send your new passport.

{% endcall %}

You can bring a friend or relative with you to the office, but they can’t sit with you during the interview.

Booking your appointment

We’ll send you a letter asking you to phone for an appointment. When you call, tell us if you need:

  • a hearing loop
  • wheelchair access
  • a sign language interpreter
  • someone to be with you during the interview, such as a parent or carer
  • a private interview room because you don’t want to uncover your face in public

Help if you have a disability

Most offices have wheelchair access and hearing loops, and some have disabled toilets and parking. Ask what facilities are available when you book.

If you have a mental or physical disability that would prevent you from coming and taking part in an interview, you’ll need to send a letter from a doctor.

The letter should explain why you can’t attend and if your disability is likely to be permanent. Include it with your documents.
